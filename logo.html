100th anniversary of the Benebekira Sisters

We designed this logo for the 100th anniversary of the Benebekira Sisters of Rwanda, who work to develop education and healthcare, and provide support for those impacted by the Rwandan genocide.

Cultural revival

In creating the logo for the Benebikira Sisters, we wanted to stay true to Rwandan culture. We were inspired by Imigongo, a traditional Rwandan art form. Today, the revival of Imigongo is helping genocide victims recover. The colors and geometric form of the dove are highly influenced by this art.

Texture is an important part of this art, leading to our decision to include overlapping shapes and colors. In addition, the flower is inspired by a Tuberose Agapanthus Gladiolus, one of the common species found in the country.

Cow Dung Artists at work

Rwandan public art installation

The most renowned Imigongo Cooperative currently employs 15 women. Almost all of them are genocide widows. Their canvasses vary from small wooden squares to customized full-wall murals. The artists will usually work collectively on a single piece. One artist will sketch a pattern on a square of wood and then trace the pattern with fingers full of cow dung. Once dry, the pattern is painted over by another artist, who uses paints made from red clay, black ash, or white kaolin, among other natural materials.

Application

Considering that education is a primary mission of the congregation, especially the education of girls, we decided that we would also design anotebook that they could distribute to the kids in their program. Since 1994 they have built four new schools of our their own to provide the opportunity for education to more children, and we are more than happy to help them celebrate this work aswell as the their positive fututre helping and supporting their communities.
